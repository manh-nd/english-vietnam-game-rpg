# Playtest Guide

Use this guide for the first external playtests of the Ha Giang Phaser web MVP. The goal is to learn whether the authored dialogue and lesson loop is clear, useful, and fun before adding larger systems.

## Target Tester

A good first tester is:

- A Vietnamese A1/A2 English learner.
- Comfortable trying a browser game for 10–15 minutes.
- Willing to say where the English, UI, or game goal feels confusing.

## Test Instructions

1. Open the shared web link, or run a local production preview.
2. Move with **WASD** or the **arrow keys**.
3. Talk to nearby NPCs by clicking them or pressing **E**.
4. Read the short dialogue.
5. Answer the English lesson choices.
6. Watch for quest, vocabulary, XP, and passport stamp feedback.

## Test Tasks

Ask the tester to try these tasks without extra explanation unless they get stuck:

1. Talk to May and finish at least 2 lessons.
2. Talk to Binh and finish at least 1 lesson.
3. Talk to Lan and finish at least 1 lesson.
4. Complete at least one quest.
5. Notice any vocabulary, XP, or passport stamp feedback.

## Feedback Questions

After the test, ask:

- Did you understand what to do?
- Was the English too easy, too hard, or the right level?
- Did the Vietnamese explanation help?
- Did it feel like a game or just a quiz?
- Which NPC felt most useful?
- Where did you feel confused?
- Would you continue playing another location?

## Learning and Fun Metrics

Ask testers to rate each item from 1 to 5:

- **Clarity:** 1 = very confusing, 5 = very clear.
- **Fun:** 1 = not fun, 5 = very fun.
- **English usefulness:** 1 = not useful, 5 = very useful.
- **Desire to continue:** 1 = would stop, 5 = wants another location.

## Observer Notes

During the test, note:

- Where the tester first hesitates.
- Whether they discover interaction controls naturally.
- Whether they read the Vietnamese explanations.
- Whether reward feedback feels noticeable.
- Which lesson or NPC creates the most positive reaction.
